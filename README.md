## Motor-Vehicle-Collision-Analytics

### Project Overview

This project delivers a comprehensive data analytics solution designed to evaluate public safety through historical road accident data. The interactive Power BI dashboard processes core parameters including accident severity, vehicle types, environmental conditions, and geographic distribution to identify high-risk patterns. The goal is to provide actionable intelligence for municipal authorities, urban planners, and traffic safety enforcement teams to mitigate casualties.

### Tools & Technologies

Business Intelligence: Power BI Desktop

Data Modeling: Power Query (ETL processing) & DAX (Time Intelligence and KPI creation)

Visualization Techniques: Multi-axis trend tracking, geospatial mapping, and breakdown donut charts

### Key Business Insights

* Primary High-Level KPIs: The system records 4,404 Reported Accidents, which directly resulted in a Total Current Year (CY) Casualty count of 3,386 across 2,221 CY Accidents.

* Severity Breakdown: The vast majority of injuries are classified as CY Slight Casualties at 2,892, followed by 463 CY Serious Casualties, and 31 CY Fatal Casualties.

* Vehicle Vulnerability: Passenger cars are involved in the overwhelming majority of incidents with 100,781 casualties, while bikes (10,438) and vans (10,012) represent the secondary high-risk vehicle tiers.

* Geographic & Environmental Risk Factors: * Urban vs. Rural: Built-up urban locations are significantly more hazardous, accounting for 66.4% of casualties compared to 33.6% in rural areas.

* Infrastructure: Single carriageway roads overwhelmingly dominate the infrastructure risk matrix, scaling toward 100K casualties.

* Visibility: Nearly three-quarters of incidents occur during daylight hours (73.37%), leaving 26.63% occurring in dark conditions.

### Strategic Recommendations

Insight: Single carriageway infrastructure accounts for the highest volume of vehicle casualties by a wide margin.

* Action: Deploy targeted infrastructure upgrades, such as physical median separators, rumble strips, and clearer lane demarcations on high-density single lanes.

Insight: Urban environments see double the casualties (66.4%) of rural networks, heavily driven by passenger cars.

* Action: Implement smart city traffic-calming measures (e.g., automated speed cameras, optimized pedestrian crossing intervals, and low-speed zones) in dense metropolitan hubs.

Insight: Daytime accidents represent 73.37% of the total profile, pointing to high-volume congestion risks rather than visibility limitations.

* Action: Increase traffic enforcement patrols and peak-hour congestion routing strategies to manage high-density commuter traffic flow safely.

Insight: Two-wheeled bikes constitute a significant vulnerable road user group with 10,438 casualties.

* Action: Invest in dedicated, physically segregated bike lanes within urban planning budgets to isolate smaller vehicles from heavy car traffic.
